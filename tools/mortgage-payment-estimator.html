Frequently Asked Questions

What is PITI in mortgage calculations?

PITI = Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance. This is the total monthly cost of homeownership. Most lenders cap your total PITI at 28% of gross monthly income for conventional loans.

How is mortgage principal vs interest split?

Early in a loan term, most payments go to interest. For a 30-year loan at 7%, roughly 65% of the first payment is interest. By year 15, it's closer to 50/50. Extra payments toward principal accelerate this split.

What down payment do I need for a conventional loan?

Conventional loans typically require 3-20% down. Putting down 20%+ eliminates private mortgage insurance (PMI) but requires stronger reserves. First-time buyers often use 3-5% with PMI, then refinance later.

How do property taxes affect my monthly payment?

Property taxes are escrowed and included in your monthly payment. They vary by location (0.5-2.5% of home value annually). The lender estimates these based on the purchase price and local tax rates.

What's the difference between a 15-year and 30-year mortgage?

A 15-year mortgage has higher monthly payments but saves tens of thousands in total interest over the life of the loan. A 30-year mortgage has lower payments but pays more interest overall.
