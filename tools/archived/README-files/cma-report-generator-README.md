## Frequently Asked Questions
### Q: What is a CMA in real estate??
**A Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) is a report that estimates a property's value based on recent sales of similar properties, current listings, and expired/withdrawn listings. It's not an appraisal — it's a marketing tool that helps agents recommend pricing and win listings.**

### Q: How is a CMA different from an appraisal??
**An appraisal is a licensed professional's opinion of value used by lenders. A CMA is an agent's analytical report used for pricing recommendations and listing presentations. CMAs are more flexible and include market context that appraisals don't.**

### Q: What makes a CMA professional??
**A professional CMA includes: a clear subject property summary, 3-6 comparable sales with adjustments, active and under-contract competition, DOM analysis, price per square foot trends, market conditions narrative, and a clear pricing recommendation with rationale.**

## Success Tips
1. Always include 3 sold comps, 3 active listings, and 3 under-contract — the magic number builds credibility
2. Use adjusted values, not just raw comparables — a $400K home with a 2-car garage vs a $400K home with a 3-car garage needs explanation
3. Include a "Why this price?" narrative section — it transforms data into a story sellers can understand and act on
