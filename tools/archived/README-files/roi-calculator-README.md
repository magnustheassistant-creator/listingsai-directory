## Frequently Asked Questions
### Q: What is a good ROI for a rental property??
**Target cash-on-cash returns of 8-12% annually for buy-and-hold properties in most markets. Cap rates of 5-8% are reasonable for residential rental properties. The best deals often achieve 15%+ cash-on-cash returns when you find motivated sellers or below-market purchases.**

### Q: What cap rate is considered good??
**For residential rentals, cap rates of 5-8% are typical. Higher cap rates (8%+) often indicate higher risk or lower quality properties. Location matters significantly — urban properties often trade at 4-6% cap while rural properties might be 10%+. Compare cap rates within similar property types and neighborhoods.**

### Q: How do you calculate ROI on a real estate investment??
**ROI = (Annual Return - Total Investment Costs) / Total Investment Costs x 100. Annual return includes rental income minus all expenses (mortgage, taxes, insurance, management, maintenance, vacancy). Total investment includes down payment, closing costs, and renovation costs.**
