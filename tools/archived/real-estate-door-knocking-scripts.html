💡 First 7 Seconds Rule

You have 7 seconds to make a positive impression. Smile, make eye contact, and lead with value before they decide to close the door.

🎯 The 3-Foot Rule

Stay 3 feet back when speaking at the door — close enough to be personable, far enough to not feel like pressure. Never step inside without invitation.

📝 Take Notes Immediately

After each door, write down what worked and what didn't. Note which streets had the best response. Build your own playbook over time.

🔁 Follow Up 3 Times

Most deals come from the 3rd–7th contact. Use a door knocking log to track every address and when to follow up via call, text, or mail.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I say when door knocking for real estate?

Start with a friendly introduction including your name and the reason you're there. For FSBOs, say 'I noticed your home is for sale by owner — many sellers find they get more showings and better offers when working with a professional.' For expired listings, reference the previous listing date and offer your expertise. Always listen more than you talk.

Are door knocking scripts actually effective for real estate agents?

Yes. Scripted approaches consistently outperform random conversation because they ensure you cover key points (property value proposition, your credentials, call to action) without forgetting anything under pressure. Scripts also build confidence so you appear more professional and less nervous at each door.

How do I get people to open the door when cold door knocking?

Wear professional attire, hold a property flyer or market report, and speak confidently within 3 seconds of approaching. Lead with a benefit: 'Hi, I'm doing a neighborhood market analysis and wanted to share some data about home values in this area.' People are more likely to engage when they see value in what you're offering.

What is the best time of day to door knock for real estate?

Saturday and Sunday mornings (9am–12pm) are best for residential areas. Weekday evenings (5–7pm) work for busy professionals. Avoid early mornings before 9am, mealtimes (12–1pm, 6–7pm), and late evenings after 8pm.

How do I handle objections during door knocking?

Use the LAER framework: Listen, Acknowledge, Explore, Respond. When someone says 'Not interested,' respond with 'I completely understand — may I ask what would make you more interested in selling your home?' This uncovers their real concern (timing, price expectation, agent relationship) so you can address it directly.
