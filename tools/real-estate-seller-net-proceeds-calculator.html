How to Maximize Your Net Proceeds

📉 Negotiate Agent Commission

Don't accept 6% by default. In a competitive market, 4-5% is realistic — especially if you're willing to do some of the marketing yourself. On a $500K home, cutting commission by 1% saves you $5,000.

💰 Price to Sell, Not to Wait

Every month your home sits on market costs you $2,000-$4,000 in carrying costs. A price that attracts multiple offers in week 1 will net you more than a higher price that sits for 90 days.

🛠️ Factor in Pre-Listing Repairs

Buyers request repairs averaging 3-5% of the sale price in negotiated concessions. Get a pre-listing inspection so you can address issues before they become negotiation leverage.

🏠 Capital Gains Exclusion

If you've lived in the home 2 of the last 5 years, you pay zero federal capital gains tax on up to $250K ($500K married). This is the most overlooked savings in home sales.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate net proceeds from selling a home?

Net proceeds = Sale Price - Mortgage Payoff - Agent Commissions - Closing Costs - Seller Concessions - Capital Gains Taxes (if applicable). The biggest variable is usually agent commissions (5-6% total) and closing costs (1-2%), which many sellers underestimate. This calculator factors in all major line items so you get an accurate number before you list.

What closing costs does a seller typically pay?

Seller closing costs typically include: title insurance (0.5-1% of sale price), transfer taxes (0.5-2% depending on state), attorney fees ($500-$1,500), prorated property taxes, homeowners association fees at closing, and any outstanding liens. In most markets, sellers pay 1-2% of the sale price in closing costs — but this varies significantly by location.

Do I have to pay capital gains tax when I sell my home?

Possibly, but the IRS allows sellers to exclude up to $250,000 of gain ($500,000 for married couples) from their taxable income if they meet two requirements: (1) You owned the home for at least 2 of the last 5 years, and (2) You lived in the home as your primary residence for at least 2 of the last 5 years. If your gain exceeds these thresholds, the excess is taxed as long-term capital gains at 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on your income bracket.

What is the average real estate agent commission?

The national average for total agent commissions is 5-6% of the sale price, typically split between the listing agent (2.5-3%) and the buyer's agent (2.5-3%). However, commissions are negotiable — there's no standard rate. Many sellers successfully negotiate commissions down to 4% or even 3% depending on the market and the agent's willingness to reduce their fee. How accurate is a net proceeds calculator?

This calculator gives a highly accurate estimate within 2-3% of your actual net proceeds, assuming you enter accurate numbers.
