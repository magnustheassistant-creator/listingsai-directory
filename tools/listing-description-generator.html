Listing Description Best Practices

🎯 Lead with the Hook

The first two sentences appear in search results. Put your best feature up front — location, recent renovation, or asking price.

📝 Be Specific, Not Generic

"Updated kitchen with quartz counters" beats "beautiful kitchen." Numbers convert: use exact bedroom/bath/sqft figures.

🏡 Include Neighborhood Context

Mention walkability, nearby amenities, school district, or commute times. Buyers want to picture their life, not just the house.

✨ End with a Call to Action

Invite buyers: "Schedule your private showing today" or "Text us for a tour." Make the next step obvious.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I write a good real estate listing description?

A great listing description highlights key features (upgrades, location benefits, square footage), uses evocative but honest language, and includes a clear call-to-action. Our AI generator creates this in seconds.

What makes a listing description convert?

The best listing descriptions are specific (beds/baths/sqft), highlight unique features, and create emotional appeal without exaggeration. They answer why this property is special.

Should listing descriptions mention price?

Typically no — price should be separate from the description. Focus on property features, location benefits, and lifestyle the home offers. Price can be mentioned if there's urgency (e.g., 'Reduced to sell').
