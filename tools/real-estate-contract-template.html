Contract Best Practices

⚖️ Always Use an Attorney

Contracts vary by state and circumstance. Use this template as a starting point and have a licensed real estate attorney review it before signing. The cost of legal review ($150–$400) is far less than a lawsuit over a botched contract.

📝 Be Specific on Contingencies

Vague contingencies lead to disputes. Specify exact timeframes (e.g., "buyer has 10 business days from execution to complete inspection"), exact amounts, and exact conditions. Ambiguity is the #1 cause of contract disputes.

💵 Protect Your Earnest Money

Never give earnest money directly to the seller — always deposit it into an escrow account (title company or attorney). This protects both parties. Specify conditions under which earnest money is refundable vs. non-refundable.

📋 Include All Property Included in Sale

List everything that conveys with the property: appliances, fixtures, landscaping, parking spaces, storage units. Use "attached fixtures" and "all existing equipment" language. Exclusions must be clearly stated in writing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are free real estate contract templates legal?

Free templates can provide guidance and starting points, but they are not a substitute for legal advice from a licensed real estate attorney. Use them to understand contract structure, then have an attorney review your specific transaction.

What is the most common real estate contract?

The Residential Purchase Agreement is the most used contract. It covers offer price, contingencies (inspection, financing, appraisal), closing date, and seller concessions. Always use your state's required forms.

Can I fill out real estate contracts myself?

You can complete certain forms yourself in some states, but real estate transactions involve significant legal and financial risk. Working with a real estate agent and attorney is strongly recommended for most transactions.
