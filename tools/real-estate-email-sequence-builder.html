Email Sequence Best Practices

⏰ Timing Matters

Send emails Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10am or 5–6pm. Avoid Monday mornings (inbox overload) and Friday afternoons (people check out early). The first email should go out within 5 minutes of capturing the lead.

📧 Subject Lines Win Opens

Your subject line is 80% of your email's success. Personalize with their address or name. Ask a question. Create curiosity. Test 3-5 subjects per sequence and drop the low open-rate ones.

🤖 Automate Everything

Set up your sequence once, then forget it. Use ManyChat or Mailchimp to trigger emails based on: form fill, link click, reply, or time delay. The best agents automate their follow-up and focus on conversations.

📊 Track & Improve

Monitor open rates (target 25%+), click rates (target 3%+), and reply rates. If your open rate is below 20%, test new subjects. If below 2% CTR, test new CTAs. Always be refining your messaging.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an email sequence in real estate?

An email sequence is a series of automated, value-driven emails sent over time to nurture leads. A typical real estate sequence includes a welcome email, market update, specific listing alert, and call-to-action — spaced 3-7 days apart.

How many emails should be in a real estate drip campaign?

Most effective drip campaigns have 6-12 emails spanning 2-4 weeks. Too few and you don't build rapport; too many and people unsubscribe. Focus on providing genuine value in each email.

How do I get email addresses for real estate marketing?

Collect emails through your website lead capture forms, open house sign-in sheets, CRM, and social media. Always get explicit consent to email and include an easy unsubscribe option to comply with CAN-SPAM laws.
