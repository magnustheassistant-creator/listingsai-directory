Frequently Asked Questions

What is lead qualification in real estate?

Lead qualification is the process of scoring prospects based on their likelihood to buy or sell. It filters out tire-kickers from serious clients, helping agents focus time on leads most likely to convert to closed deals.

What are the key factors in lead scoring?

The 6 key factors are: Budget (can they afford to buy/sell?), Timeline (when are they looking to act?), Financing (pre-approved? cash?), Motivation (why now?), Agent History (working with an agent already?), and Source (how did they find you?).

What is a hot lead score in real estate?

A hot lead scores 70+ and typically has pre-approval, a defined timeline within 60 days, strong motivation, and is not already working with another agent. These leads need immediate follow-up within 24 hours.

How often should I re-score my leads?

Re-score leads every 2-3 weeks or after any significant interaction. A lead's score can change quickly — a cold prospect who gets pre-approved becomes hot overnight. Keep your pipeline fresh.

What's the best follow-up strategy for warm leads?

For warm leads (score 40-69): call within 48 hours, send a personalized market report for their criteria, offer a free valuation or buyer consultation. Focus on building trust and demonstrating local market expertise.
