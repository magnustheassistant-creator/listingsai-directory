Why Most Agents Qualify Leads Wrong

Most agents qualify by "gut feel." They talk to a lead for 20 minutes, get a good vibe, and assume they're serious. Then two weeks later, the lead goes dark with no explanation — or worse, buys from another agent while they're still sending listing emails.

The problem is that gut feel is inconsistent and easily manipulated. A smooth talker with no money can make you feel confident. A serious buyer who's just quiet can feel like a waste of time.

The solution is a scoring system. Six factors, each scored objectively, added up to give you a number. That number tells you exactly what to do.

"The agents making $200K+ a year don't work harder — they qualify faster and focus only on leads that can actually close."

The 6-Factor Lead Qualification Score

Here's the complete rubric. Each lead scores 0-100 on these six factors:

Factor What to Ask Score Range
Budget "What's your budget or price range?" 0–25 pts
Timeline "When are you looking to buy/sell?" 0–24 pts
Financing "Are you pre-approved or pre-qualified?" 0–15 pts
Motivation "How actively are you looking right now?" 0–20 pts
Lead Source "How did you find me / reach out?" 0–15 pts
Has Agent "Are you currently working with an agent?" 0 to –15 pts

Budget Scoring (0–25 points)

Budget is the single most important factor. A lead with a $200K budget and financing ready is more valuable than a "might buy in a year" lead with a $2M budget.

  • Pre-approved + proof of funds: 25 points
  • Pre-approved amount in range: 20 points
  • Expressed range (not pre-approved): 10–15 points (based on realism of range)
  • Vague / "depends": 0–5 points

Timeline Scoring (0–24 points)

Urgency matters more than desire. A lead who "needs to move in 60 days" is worth 10x more than one who "might buy sometime."

  • Within 1–3 months: 24 points
  • 3–6 months: 15 points
  • 6–12 months: 8 points
  • 12+ months: 3 points
  • "Just looking" / no timeline: 0 points

Financing Scoring (0–15 points)

There's a massive difference between "I have a letter" and "I've been approved."

  • Pre-approved + proof of funds: 15 points
  • Pre-approved only: 10 points
  • Pre-qualification letter: 5 points
  • Cash buyer: 15 points
  • Unknown / need to discuss: -10 points

Motivation Scoring (0–20 points)

Are they actually doing something, or just thinking about it?

  • Actively touring homes: 20 points
  • Has an agent, touring soon: 15 points
  • Working with an agent: 10 points
  • Just started looking: 5 points
  • "Getting a feel for the market": -10 points

Lead Source Scoring (0–15 points)

Where they came from predicts how likely they are to work with you.

  • Referral from past client: 15 points
  • Referral from agent/partner: 10 points
  • Zillow / Realtor.com inquiry: 8 points
  • Open house sign-in: 5 points
  • Social media / website: 3 points
  • Cold outreach / random lead gen: -5 points

Agent Status Scoring (0 to –15 points)

This one's simple — if they already have a committed agent, you're fighting an uphill battle.

  • No agent — working with you: 20 points
  • Interviewing agents: 15 points
  • Has an agent — undecided: 0 points
  • Committed to another agent: -15 points

Common Questions

What if a lead scores hot but isn't actually ready?

Hot scores can be inflated by a lead who's enthusiastic but not serious. Watch for timeline drift — if someone scores hot on budget and motivation but their timeline keeps pushing out, they may be a warm lead who got an initial burst of energy. Re-score them every 2 weeks.

Should I ignore cold leads completely?

Never ignore a lead — but also never chase one manually. Cold leads go into a passive nurture system: a monthly newsletter, a quarterly market update, an annual "thinking of you" text. When they eventually transact — and they will — you want to be the agent they remember. Automate this. Don't do it yourself.

What if a lead won't answer the qualification questions?

If someone won't answer basic questions after you've explained you need the info to help them, that's itself a data point. A lead who won't engage in qualification is rarely a hot lead. Score them as 0 and put them in the cold nurture track. Either they'll come around, or you'll save weeks of wasted time.

Is a referral always a hot lead?

Almost always — but not always. A referral from a past client is your best source (15/15). A referral from someone who doesn't know you well is worth less (10/15). Always ask the referral source how motivated the person is before assuming.
