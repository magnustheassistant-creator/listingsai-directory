💡 Ways to Maximize Your Take-Home

Negotiate Your Split

Once you hit $100K+ in gross commissions, ask your broker for a better split. Going from 70/30 to 80/20 on $200K GCI means $10K more per year.

Consider Cap Plans

High-volume agents save thousands with cap plans. Pay a flat fee ($500-$1,000) after hitting the cap instead of ongoing percentage splits.

Track Every Expense

Mileage, marketing, education, software — track everything. Real estate agents can deduct home office, health insurance, and retirement contributions.

Build Volume

Two deals at $400K each with 70/30 split = ~$17K net. Four deals = ~$34K. Volume is the fastest way to offset desk fees and franchise costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is real estate commission typically split?
The total commission (usually 5-6% of the sale price) is first split between the seller's agent and buyer's agent. Each agent then splits their portion with their broker. A typical split is 70/30 or 80/20 for newer agents, while top producers may get 90/10 or 100% commission. Franchise fees, MLS fees, and other costs are also deducted from your take-home.
What is a standard real estate agent commission split?
Industry standard is 70/30 to 80/20 between agent and broker. Some brokers charge a flat fee per transaction instead of a percentage. At 70/30, if the total commission is 6% and the home sells for $400,000, the total commission is $24,000. Each agent's half is $12,000, and after the 70/30 split, the agent takes home $8,400 before expenses.
Do I have to pay franchise fees as a real estate agent?
It depends on your brokerage. Franchise brokers (like RE/MAX, Keller Williams, Coldwell Banker) typically charge 6-10% of your gross commission as a franchise/desk fee. Some brokers have no franchise fees but take a higher percentage split. Always ask about all fees before joining a brokerage.
How much does an agent actually take home on a $500,000 sale?
On a $500,000 home with 6% total commission and a 70/30 split: total commission is $30,000. Each side gets $15,000. After the 70/30 broker split, the agent keeps $10,500. After franchise fees (8%), MLS ($45), and other costs, the net take-home is approximately $8,500-$9,000 depending on your specific fee structure.
What are MLS fees for real estate agents?
MLS (Multiple Listing Service) fees typically range from $0 to $100 per transaction, or annual subscriptions from $200-$500. Some brokers pass this cost to agents; others cover it. It's usually one of the smaller deductions in your commission breakdown.
How can I increase my net commission as an agent?
Negotiate a better split with your broker (especially after hitting production thresholds), minimize franchise and desk fees by choosing the right brokerage, track and deduct all legitimate business expenses, build volume to offset per-transaction fees, and consider capping your desk fees at a certain amount per year.
